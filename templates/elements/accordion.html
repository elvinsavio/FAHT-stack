How much is the tax?

Every punchline costs one copper coin. A full jest with setup and delivery is taxed at three coins. The jester's guild has formally protested.

Who enforces the Joke Tax?

The Royal Tax Collectors, known as the Humorless Guard, patrol the streets listening for laughter and issuing fines on the spot.

Can I appeal my joke fine?

Appeals may be filed at the Bureau of Wit and Taxation. However, the bureau chief has not laughed since 1987 and denies all appeals on principle.

FAQ about king tax on jokes

Why are jokes being taxed?

The king needed revenue and noticed people laughing too much for free. A royal economist calculated that the average citizen laughed 47 times a day, which was deemed an untapped goldmine.

How much does each joke cost?

A simple pun is taxed at one copper coin. A full joke with setup and punchline costs three coins. Wordplay involving the royal family is subject to a luxury surcharge.

Are knock-knock jokes exempt?

No. Knock-knock jokes are actually double-taxed due to their repetitive nature and the kingdom's Anti-Redundancy in Humor Act of 1204.

What happens if I laugh without paying?

The Humorless Guard will issue you a Grimace Citation. Repeat offenders are sentenced to perform stand-up comedy for the king, who has not smiled since the Great Pun Famine.

Can I buy a joke subscription?

Yes. The Royal Bureau of Wit offers monthly plans starting at 50 coins, which covers unlimited puns, two slapstick incidents, and one satirical ballad per week.

Still have questions? Visit the Royal Bureau of Wit and Taxation, open weekdays except Jester's Holiday.
